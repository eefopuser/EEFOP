of Pendataktylos Mountains
Brassica Hilarionis Delphinium Caseyi Onosma Caespitosa Salvia Veneris Sideritis Cypria Silenopis Antiphonitis Teucrium Kyreniae

Solenopsis antiphonitis Hadjik. & Hand

CAMPANULACEAE


Summary: Acaulescent, slender annual herb. Leaves rosulate, broadly ovate or suborbicular, 2-8 × 1.5-7 mm; base abruptly narrowed into a petiole 4-11 mm long. Floral pedicels 14-40 mm long, lengthening to 5.5 cm in fruit. Calyx with 5 linear-lanceolate lobes 1.3-1.7 mm long. Corolla 5-6.5 mm long, bilabiate, upper lip 2-lobed and lower 3-lobed; tube 3-3.5 mm long; lobes white internally, tinged lilac towards the margins, throat yellow. Capsule bilocular, ellipsoid, 2-3 × 2-2.5 mm. Endemic of Cyprus (discovered in 2005), confined to 2 locations on the Pentadaktylos range (about 5 and 7 km east of Antifonitis Monastery respectively), on wet streambeds (5.2) with shallow soil and temporary water flow, at an altitude of 200- 280 m; the larger subpopulation consists of approximately 1,500 plants and the second of about 300 plants (measured in 2005). In 2006, a substantial reduction in the population size was observed (G. Hadjikyriakou 2006, pers. comm.). Threatened by prolonged drought and possibly by road construction.


IUCN: Endangered
