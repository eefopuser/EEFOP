of Pendataktylos Mountains
Brassica Hilarionis Delphinium Caseyi Onosma Caespitosa Salvia Veneris Sideritis Cypria Silenopis Antiphonitis Teucrium Kyreniae

Delphinium caseyi B.L. Burtt D. fissum Waldst.
& Kit. subsp. caseyi (B.L. Burtt) C. Blanché & J. Molero

RANUNCULACEAE


Summary: Erect perennial, hairy herb, 50-85 cm high, with a woody rootstock. Basal leaves reniforn, palmately divided into 3-lobed segments. Flowers hairy, spurred violet or violet-purple. Endemic to Cyprus, confined to 2 locations on the Pendataktylos range: Kyparissovouno (30 plants) and near Agios Ilarion Castle (50 plants), in fissures of limestone rocks, in open garigue and maquis, on mountain summits (6), at altitude 800-950 m. The entire population is found in state forest land, which has been selected, based on scientific criteria, to be proposed as a Natura 2000 site; however it has not been proposed yet. Threatened by military activities of the Turkish troops (10.6 & 1.8) and grazing (8.2). The subpopulation at Kyparissovouno has suffered considerable damage from the erection of huge telecommunication antennae on the peak (1.4.7). Also threatened by stochastic environmental and demographic changes due to small size and localisation (7, 9.5 & 9.9). [CR: C2a(i)]


IUCN: Critically Endangered
